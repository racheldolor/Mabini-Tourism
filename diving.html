Welcome to Mabini's Diving Paradise

Mabini, particularly Anilao, is renowned worldwide as one of the best diving destinations in Southeast Asia. With crystal-clear waters, vibrant coral reefs, and an abundance of marine life, divers of all skill levels can enjoy unforgettable underwater adventures. The ideal diving season is from November to May when the waters are calmest and visibility is at its best.

Popular Diving Spots

Discover the best underwater locations Mabini has to offer

Anilao East

The most popular diving site featuring pristine coral formations and abundant marine life. Perfect for intermediate to advanced divers.

Depth: 15-40 meters | Level: Intermediate

Layag Point

A site known for its strong currents and pelagic encounters. Home to rays, barracudas, and occasionally larger fish species.

Depth: 20-50 meters | Level: Advanced

Barrio Barretto

An ideal site for beginners with shallow waters and colorful reef fish. Great for underwater photography with excellent visibility.

Depth: 5-25 meters | Level: Beginner

Cathedral

Named after its towering rock formations that resemble a cathedral. Features vibrant coral gardens and diverse marine life.

Depth: 12-35 meters | Level: Intermediate

Fish Sanctuary

A protected marine reserve teeming with schooling fish and large sea creatures. Perfect for observing marine biodiversity.

Depth: 8-30 meters | Level: Beginner

Twin Rocks

Two distinctive underwater formations create an exciting diving experience with strong currents and encounter opportunities.

Depth: 15-40 meters | Level: Intermediate

Diving Facts & Information

  • Mabini has over 30 established diving sites suitable for all skill levels
  • The water temperature ranges from 26-29°C (79-84°F) year-round
  • Visibility typically ranges from 15-40 meters, with 40+ meters during optimal conditions
  • Home to over 500 species of fish and 350 species of corals
  • Many dive shops offer PADI certification courses at all levels
  • Muck diving and macro photography are also popular in certain areas
  • Night diving opportunities available with experienced guides
  • Marine protected areas help ensure sustainable diving practices

Best Time to Dive

Dry Season (November to May): This is the ideal time for diving in Mabini. The seas are calmer, visibility is excellent, and the weather is more predictable. Peak season is from December to February.

Monsoon Season (June to October): While diving is still possible, seas can be rougher and visibility may be reduced. Some sites may be inaccessible during this period.

Practical Diving Information

  • Minimum certification: Open Water Diver (for most sites)
  • Dive costs typically range from ₱1,500 to ₱2,500 per dive
  • Equipment rental available at all dive shops
  • Most dives are morning (6-7 AM) and afternoon (1-2 PM) dives
  • Local dive masters are experienced and knowledgeable about conditions
  • Decompression chambers available in nearby Batangas City
  • Travel insurance and dive insurance are recommended
