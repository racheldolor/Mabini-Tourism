Discover the Magic of Snorkeling

Snorkeling is one of the most accessible ways to explore the underwater beauty of Mabini. No certification required! Simply put on your mask, snorkel, and fins to witness the incredible coral reefs and marine life that lie just beneath the surface. Mabini's warm, clear waters and shallow reef systems make it an ideal destination for snorkelers of all ages.

Best Snorkeling Spots

Accessible locations perfect for snorkeling adventures

Sepoc Point

A shallow reef perfect for beginners with vibrant corals and colorful tropical fish. Excellent for first-time snorkelers and families.

Depth: 1-8 meters | Difficulty: Easy

Anilao Reef

A diverse reef system with healthy corals and abundant marine life. Great visibility and easy access from the shore.

Depth: 2-12 meters | Difficulty: Easy-Moderate

Dry Hand Island

An island snorkeling destination with pristine coral formations and schools of fish. Includes beach access and rest areas.

Depth: 1-10 meters | Difficulty: Easy

Mainit Point

A scenic snorkeling location featuring diverse marine species and interesting rock formations. Protected by local conservation efforts.

Depth: 2-15 meters | Difficulty: Moderate

Tinglad Island

A beautiful island destination with spectacular snorkeling around submerged rock formations. Home to rays, jacks, and groupers.

Depth: 2-18 meters | Difficulty: Moderate

Mabini Beach

Beach snorkeling with easy access and calm waters. Perfect for families and those wanting a relaxed snorkeling experience.

Depth: 1-6 meters | Difficulty: Very Easy

Why Snorkel in Mabini?

  • No certification or prior experience required - perfect for all ages
  • Shallow reef systems ideal for viewing marine life from the surface
  • Crystal-clear waters with excellent visibility of 20-40 meters
  • Rich coral formations hosting diverse fish and marine species
  • Year-round snorkeling opportunities with best conditions November-May
  • Affordable compared to diving - starting at ₱500 for guided tours
  • Family-friendly and accessible to people of all fitness levels
  • Perfect for underwater photography with minimal gear

    • Marine Life You'll Encounter

    When snorkeling in Mabini, you'll see incredible varieties of marine life including:

    • Reef Fish: Parrotfish, angelfish, damselfish, and wrasses
    • Larger Fish: Barracudas, jacks, and occasional groupers
    • Rays and Sharks: Stingrays, eagle rays, and harmless reef sharks
    • Sea Turtles: Green and hawksbill turtles (seasonal)
    • Invertebrates: Sea stars, sea urchins, and octopuses
    • Corals: Hard corals, soft corals, and anemones
    • Crustaceans: Crabs and lobsters in reef crevices

    ✓ DO's

    • Use reef-safe sunscreen to protect coral ecosystems
    • Wear UV-protective clothing and rash guards
    • Stay in groups and never snorkel alone
    • Keep a safe distance from marine wildlife
    • Listen to your guide's instructions
    • Practice proper breathing techniques before going deep
    • Take breaks and stay hydrated
    • Respect marine protection areas

    ✕ DON'Ts

    • Don't touch or stand on coral - it damages the ecosystem
    • Don't chase or pursue marine animals
    • Don't feed fish or other marine life
    • Don't wear sunscreen that harms coral
    • Don't collect shells or marine specimens
    • Don't snorkel under the influence
    • Don't venture too far from shore if inexperienced
    • Don't ignore weather warnings or water conditions

    Best Time to Snorkel

    Ideal Season (November to May): These months offer calm seas, excellent visibility, and stable weather. December to February is peak season with the best conditions.

    Off-Season (June to October): Snorkeling is still possible but seas may be rougher and visibility can be reduced. Some areas may be less accessible.

    Best Time of Day: Early morning (6-10 AM) offers the best visibility and calmest waters. Afternoon visits are also good but currents may be stronger.

    What to Bring

    • Mask and snorkel (or rent at local shops)
    • Fins/flippers for easier movement
    • Rash guard or UV protective clothing
    • Reef-safe sunscreen (SPF 50+)
    • Water bottle and snacks
    • Towel and change of clothes
    • Underwater camera to capture memories
    • Cash for entrance fees and tips
