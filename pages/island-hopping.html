🏝️ Island Hopping in Mabini

Hop across sandbars, coves, and pristine islands by traditional bangka. Swim, snorkel, picnic, and soak in Mabini's coastal scenery in one unforgettable day.

What is Island Hopping?

Island hopping strings together multiple beaches, coves, and sandbars in a single boat trip. It blends swimming, snorkeling, beach time, and food stops—perfect for groups and relaxed explorers.

Why Mabini?

Mabini offers calm waters, short travel times between spots, and rich marine life near shore. Clear turquoise water, dramatic coastlines, and nearby islets make every stop photogenic and easy to reach.

Best For

Families, friend groups, couples, casual snorkelers, and anyone who wants a scenic, low-effort day on the water.

Featured Island Stops

Mix of sandbars, snorkeling coves, and photo-perfect shores—all within short hops.

Sombrero Island

Iconic hat-shaped island with white sand and reef just offshore. Great for swimming, sunbathing, and group photos.

⏱️ ~25 min boat ride 📍 Swim + Snorkel

Sepoc Beach

Quiet cove with golden sand and calm water. Ideal for picnics, beach games, and relaxed dips.

⏱️ ~20 min boat ride 📍 Picnic + Chill

Maricaban Island

Crystal-clear shallows with coral patches close to shore. Great mix of snorkeling and shoreline lounging.

⏱️ ~30 min boat ride 📍 Snorkel + Beach

Anilao Sandbar

Shallow sandbar that appears at low tide—perfect for drone shots, wading, and sunset watching.

⏱️ ~15 min boat ride 📍 Wading + Photos

Your Island Hopping Day

How a typical half- or full-day boat trip flows.

1️⃣

Meet & Safety Briefing

Gather at the resort or marina. Guides review life vests, snorkeling basics, and the day's route based on tide and weather.

2️⃣

First Stop: Swim & Snorkel

Head to a calm cove for a warm-up swim. Spot coral gardens and reef fish in shallow water.

3️⃣

Beach Picnic Break

Drop anchor near a beach for snacks or lunch. Lounge under shade, play frisbee, and hydrate.

4️⃣

Second Stop: Sandbar or Island

Visit a sandbar for wading and photos or another island for more snorkeling and chill time.

5️⃣

Optional Third Stop

Weather and time permitting, add a bonus site (e.g., Maricaban). Guides tailor based on your group's pace.

6️⃣

Sunset Return

Slow cruise back to shore. Rinse gear, change, and enjoy golden-hour views of Mabini's coast.

Essential Island Hopping Tips

  • Bring dry bags — protect phones, cameras, and clothes from splashes.
  • Use reef-safe sunscreen — apply 20 minutes before boarding to protect corals.
  • Hydrate often — bring at least 1–2L water per person; the sun is strong on the water.
  • Wear aqua shoes — helpful for rocky entries and hot sandbars.
  • Follow guide instructions — respect marine sanctuaries and local regulations.
  • Pack light snacks — fruits, sandwiches, and trail mix keep energy up between stops.
  • Check tides & weather — sandbar visibility and stop sequence depend on conditions.
  • Use a rash guard — extra sun protection during long snorkel sessions.
